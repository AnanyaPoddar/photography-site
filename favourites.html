OBJECT PHOTOGRAPHY

Splash Photography

Timing (and a bit of luck) was the key to capturing this photo.

I used a macro lens for a nicer close-up, a dark background to create contrast, and high shutter speed to freeze the motion of the water being poured.

Dramatic Chess Pieces

I wanted to experiment with reflections and add a flair of drama and express a sort of "power dynamic" with the chess pieces.

I set up glass chess pieces atop a glass board to get the reflections, and used black background with a single lamp for backlighting.

More Splash Photography

The goal was to freeze and capture the details of something as mundane as water.

I used a macro lens for the close-up, a dark background to create contrast, and a high shutter speed to freeze the motion.

Prompt: Create a photo given two random objects

Given a small souvenir horse carriage and a white towel, I wanted to see if I could give off the illusion of the horse going through sand.

Sunlight through a white curtain was used to backlight the souvenir and create the silhouette.

White on White on White

The goal was to learn how to use lighting to my advantage to add dimension and visual interest when the products are all the same colour.

Using diffused natural lighting (sunlight through a white curtain) worked the best.
