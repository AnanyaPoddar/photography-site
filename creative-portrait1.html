PEOPLE PHOTOGRAPHY

A creative take on traditional portrait photography: experimenting with different lighting and moods when photographing people.

Hover over each card for more info!

Edge Lighting

The goal was to add a flair of drama and mystery to a simple portrait by letting the facial features fall into shadow.

To achieve the edge lighting, a single spotlight was placed behind the subject, and a completely black background was used.

Playing With Silhouettes

The goal was to experiment with and embrace the simplicity of silhouettes by playing around with the paper butterfly prop an a pink background.

To do this, a spotlight was placed in front of the subject with a pinkish hue to shine onto the white background.

Prompt: Create a portrait shot using a household item (bristol board).

This photograph was inspired by the iconic photo from the Shining. I wanted to incorporate the bristol board in an unusual and humorous way.

Because this portrait is a close up, diffused lighting was used (using a soft box) to eliminate harsh shadows on the face.

Shoot: Dancers Edition

The goal was to create a stage-like effect in the studio, and to incorporate movement and add visual interest, I added a scarf to be thrown up in the air. This made timing and a fast shutter speed crucial.

Dark background elements were used to create contrast, and overhead spot lighting was used to create a stage spotlight effect.
